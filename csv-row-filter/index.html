How it works

How to Filter Rows in a CSV

Everything runs in your browser. No account, no software, no data leaving your device.

1

Paste CSV data or upload a file

Paste comma-separated data into the input box or upload a CSV file using the upload area.

2

Choose header and delimiter

Tell the tool whether the first row is a header and choose the delimiter if your CSV uses tabs, semicolons, or pipes.

3

Add one or more filter conditions

Pick a column, an operator, and a value. You can add multiple conditions and choose whether to match all of them or any of them.

4

Why use this tool

Why Use a CSV Row Filter?

A CSV row filter helps you pull out just the records you care about without touching the rest of the file. Instead of manually scanning and deleting rows in a spreadsheet, you can keep matching rows or remove unwanted ones in a single step.

Filter by any column

Use header names or generic column labels to filter rows by city, status, ID, or any other field.

Combine multiple conditions

Match all conditions (AND) or any condition (OR) and quickly narrow down large datasets.

Keep or remove matches

Choose whether to keep rows that match your filters or remove them from the output.

You can use it to keep only paid orders, active users, rows from a specific city, or values above a numeric threshold.
